## The Vision in One Paragraph

Prediction Trade is becoming the go-to social platform for people who think in probabilities. It's where tech enthusiasts, sports fans, crypto natives, internet culture addicts, and curious minds come to put their opinions on the line — and build a public track record of being right. Every prediction is a conversation. Every outcome is content. Every user is a potential forecasting creator.

---

## Platform Identity

### Personality Traits

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Modern** | Clean UI, dark mode default, card-based layout |
| **Visual** | Probability displayed as gradient bars, not numbers alone |
| **Social** | Every prediction is shareable. Profiles are public by default |
| **Intelligent** | AI explains markets, flags trends, summarizes outcomes |
| **Fun** | Streaks, badges, arcade mode, leaderboard |
| **AI-native** | AI is embedded in every core experience, not bolted on |
| **Viral** | Shareable cards, outcome reveals, "I was right" moments |
| **Accessible** | A 16-year-old should understand any market in 10 seconds |

### Anti-Traits (what PT should never feel like)

- Heavy / broker-like / financial anxiety
- Gambling / addictive dark patterns
- Enterprise / B2B / corporate
- Political / divisive / partisan
- Overwhelming / information overload

## Positioning Statement

**For:** Curious, internet-native people aged 16–35 who enjoy being opinionated about the future.

**Who:** Want a fun, social way to engage with current events, tech trends, sports, and culture.

**PT is:** A social forecasting platform where you predict the future, build a track record, and compete with friends.

**Unlike:** Traditional prediction markets (complex, DeFi, financial), PT is visual, social, educational, and free.

**Our difference:** PT makes forecasting a cultural activity — a skill to develop and a reputation to build.

## Target User Personas

### 1. The Curious Follower
- Age 18–28, follows tech/crypto/sports news on X and TikTok
- Wants to feel smart by predicting things before they happen
- Entry hook: "What's the AI community saying about GPT-5?"
- Retention: Streak, badge when first correct prediction

### 2. The Competitive Gamer
- Age 16–30, competitive by nature, loves leaderboards
- Entry hook: Leaderboard, "You're #47 globally"
- Retention: Weekly ranking, category championships

### 3. The Forecasting Creator
- Age 20–35, has an audience on X or YouTube, wants to monetize their opinion
- Entry hook: Public prediction record, shareable performance card
- Retention: Creator profile, "Top Predictor" badge, audience building

### 4. The Internet Culture Native
- Age 16–26, lives on memes, creator drama, gaming drops
- Entry hook: Markets about their favorite topic (gaming, streaming)
- Retention: Trending markets in their category, viral prediction cards

## Brand Voice

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Market description | Clear, engaging, no jargon | "Will GPT-5 launch before June?" |
| AI explanations | Smart but casual | "The market moved 12 points — here's why" |
| Win/lose moments | Energetic, fun | "Called it. 🎯 You were right." |
| Onboarding | Warm, encouraging | "Your first prediction. No pressure — this is how it starts." |
| Error states | Friendly, not alarming | "Something went sideways. We're on it." |

---

## Visual Direction

- **Dark mode first** — feels modern, reduces visual fatigue
- **Card-based layout** — predictions are cards, outcomes are reveals
- **Color by probability** — green high confidence, orange uncertain, red low
- **Motion** — subtle animations for probability changes, countdown timers
- **Typography** — large numbers, bold probabilities, minimal prose
- **Shareable cards** — every prediction generates a card with: market title, user prediction, probability, username

## Success Metrics (Qualitative)

A successful PT session should feel like:
- Scrolling Twitter and discovering something interesting
- Competing in a leaderboard game with friends
- Learning something real about how the world works
- Creating content that people actually engage with

If it feels like using a trading platform — something went wrong.
